Introduction

01

I trained as an architect and an engineer, then came back to painting. That training shows in how I work: composition first, a steady eye, nothing wasted. What I bring to it is warmth. A wedding watercolour should feel like the day rather than record it.

I paint couples and the people around them in a warm, filmic palette, on paper made to last. Some pieces happen live, in the room, as the night carries on. Others I paint quietly in the studio, from the photographs you love most.

How it works

Two ways in. Both unhurried.

Live on the day

I attend your wedding and paint as it happens.

  1. We talk it throughYou tell me about the day, the venue, and the people who matter. A deposit holds your date.
  2. I paint in the roomI arrive early, set up quietly, and work through the celebration while your guests watch the portrait appear.
  3. You take it homeThe piece is usually finished on the night. Anything left to refine, I complete in the studio and return to you.

Commissioned from photographs

I paint in the studio, from the images you send.

  1. Send me your photographsA few well-lit, high-resolution images that look like you. I will help you choose the one to paint from.
  2. I paint in the studioI work from your images on archival paper, building the portrait by hand over a few weeks.
  3. It arrives ready to hangYour finished watercolour is delivered with a high-resolution scan for your records.
