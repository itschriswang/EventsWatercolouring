Watercolour · 2025

The Artist

I draw what cameras flatten.

I've been painting people in watercolour since I was a teenager, putting it down for years, and picking it back up again with more intention. The gap made me more certain about what I actually want to make.

A watercolour portrait doesn't compete with photography. It reads differently — looser, more felt, more present tense. That's the point of having both.

I work with natural light, wet-on-wet washes, and a limited palette so the work stays cohesive across an event or a series of commissions. Couples tend to book me when they want something on their wall that isn't a photo.

What I Offer

01

Live event painting

I set up quietly at your venue and work through the event — ceremony, reception, or both. Most live sessions run 3–4 hours. The finished piece stays with you at the end of the night, dry and ready to take home.

From $900 · Booking lead time: 4 weeks

Commissioned portrait

Painted from your photographer's images post-event, or from reference photos you supply. I share a colour study before starting so you can see the palette before the final piece begins. 2–3 weeks to delivery.

From $450 · Booking lead time: 2 weeks

03

Bridal party suite

A set of five or more individual portraits painted from photos, designed as a cohesive series. Works as a gift for the wedding party, or as a formal record of the event's principal subjects. Mounted and presented as a collection.

I confirm the brief

I'll let you know what's feasible live on the day versus what works better as a post-event commission from reference photos. I'll also share a small colour study so you can see the palette before we commit.
