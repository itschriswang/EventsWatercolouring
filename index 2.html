How it works

Two ways in. Both unhurried.

Live on the day

I come to your wedding and paint as it happens.

  1. 1We say helloYou tell me about the day, the venue, and the people who matter. A deposit holds your date.
  2. 2I paint in the roomI arrive early, set up quietly, and paint your guests through the celebration while they watch.
  3. 3Everyone takes one homeGuests leave with their own little painting. You keep the couple portrait, framed.

From your photographs

A studio commission, with no painter at the reception.

  1. 1Send me your photosA few well-lit, high-resolution images that look like you. I will help you choose the one to paint from.
  2. 2I paint in the studioI work from your images on cotton-rag paper, building the portrait by hand over a few weeks.
  3. 3It arrives ready to hangYour finished watercolour is delivered with a high-resolution scan for your records.

Questions

The practical bits.

I take a limited number of weddings each season, so 6 to 12 months ahead is ideal. Shorter notice is sometimes possible, so reach out and I will let you know what I have open.

Both. The three packages are all painted live at your wedding. If you would rather a studio commission from your own photographs, with no painter at the reception, I am glad to do that too, priced on enquiry.

Four to six hours depending on the package, usually through the reception. Guests are welcome to watch their portrait take shape, which tends to become a quiet highlight of the evening.

Around 16 to 28 keepsake pieces depending on the package, covering roughly 32 to 56 guests when I group a few people together. I quote this as a chosen subset of the room, painted well, rather than a rushed attempt at everyone.

Professional watercolours on archival, acid-free cotton-rag paper. Everything is made to last, with lightfast pigments that hold their colour for decades when cared for.

Guest keepsakes are usually finished on the night and go home with your guests. The framed couple portrait follows within 2 to 3 weeks. Studio commissions from photographs arrive within 4 to 6 weeks.

Travel within Greater Sydney is included. Anywhere further, including interstate and destination weddings, I am glad to discuss. Just include your venue in your enquiry and I will quote travel for you.

Yes. I can license a digital file of your portrait for save-the-dates, invitations, menus, and thank-you cards. Just ask and I will include it in your quote.

A 25% deposit holds your date, with the balance due 4 weeks before the wedding. I only confirm a booking once the deposit is received, so your date is yours from that point.

Weddings move, and I understand that. If you postpone, your deposit transfers to a new date subject to my availability. The deposit is non-refundable, as it reserves time I turn away other couples to hold. Full terms are set out in your booking agreement.

The clearer the photographs, the better the portrait. Send a few well-lit, high-resolution images that show your faces clearly, and let me know which one feels most like you. I will guide you from there.
