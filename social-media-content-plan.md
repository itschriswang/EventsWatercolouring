## 1. Why this niche gets traction (and where to point effort)

Live-painting content over-indexes on watch time and shares because it's inherently
satisfying to watch (process + reveal) and inherently shareable (people tag themselves in
the guest portraits). The highest-leverage formats for this business, in order:

1. **Reels of the painting process** — time-lapse or real-time clips of a portrait going from
   blank paper to finished piece. This is the single best-performing format for live artists;
   it needs no caption to work and autoplay carries it.
2. **The reveal / reaction** — a guest seeing their finished portrait for the first time.
   Emotion carries reach; this is what gets duetted, shared to friends, and saved.
3. **Carousels that teach** — "how live wedding painting actually works," pricing breakdowns,
   before/after of the easel. Carousels drive saves and profile visits, which is what
   converts into enquiries, even if they reach fewer people than Reels.
4. **The founder story** — architect-to-painter is a genuinely unusual, scroll-stopping hook.
   Under-used on the current site copy; lean into it on video.
5. **Social proof in the wild** — guests posting their own portrait, screenshotted and
   reposted to Stories, does more for trust than any polished post.

Cadence to aim for: 3–4 Reels/week, 1 carousel/week, daily Stories during and after events.
Reels drive discovery; carousels and Stories convert discovery into enquiries.

## 3. Post ideas

### Process

1. **60-second time-lapse of one portrait, start to blank sleeve.** Trending audio, no
   voiceover needed. Caption: "One guest, one glass of wine, one portrait. About eight
   minutes, painted live at [Couple]'s wedding." — Reel.
2. **"Paint 5 guests in the time it takes to say I do."** Rapid-cut montage of five different
   portraits at 5–10 minutes each, sped to a beat drop. Strong hook for non-followers.
3. **ASMR / no-music close-up**: brush loading pigment, first wash hitting cotton paper,
   the paper's texture catching light. Post as a second version of the same event footage —
   ASMR variants often reach a completely different audience than the trend-audio cut.
4. **"Which one do you think came first?"** Two portraits of the same face side by side —
   a 5-minute quick study vs. a 10-minute detailed one. Drives comments (great for reach).
5. **Corporate activation speed round**: a brand event where you're painting a queue of
   guests back to back. Shows range beyond weddings — useful for B2B leads.

### Reveal

6. **Guest sees their finished portrait for the first time**, filmed candidly, portrait
   turned around into frame at the last second. Caption: "This is the bit I never get tired
   of." — Reel.
7. **The couple's portrait unveiled to them mid-reception**, guests gathering around.
   Caption: "Painted the couple first, while the night was still fresh." — Reel.
8. **Slow pan across a table of finished, sleeved portraits at the end of the night** —
   visual proof of volume ("24 keepsakes, one evening"). Static or Reel.

### Behind the brand

9. **"I used to design buildings. Now I paint the people at your wedding."** Direct-to-camera
   or voiceover over studio B-roll — the architect pivot is the strongest hook you have and
   it's barely used on the current site. Reel.
10. **A day in the life, wedding edition**: arrive 15 minutes early → set up → first portrait
    → last portrait → pack down. Fast cuts, on-screen captions, no dialogue needed.
11. **What's in the kit**: pans across the travel case — cotton paper, pigments, sleeves,
    water pot. Satisfying "gear tour" format that also quietly signals professionalism
    (materials quality is a real objection-handler for couples comparing artists).
12. **Studio commission time-lapse**: a portrait painted from a couple's photo weeks after
    the wedding, contrasted against the live-event footage — shows both service paths exist.

### Proof

13. **Carousel: "What 3 hours of live painting actually looks like."** Slide 1 hook, slides
    2–5 real photos from one wedding (setup → painting → guests gathered → sleeved stack),
    final slide CTA to enquire.
14. **Repost a guest's own Story/post of their portrait** (with permission) straight to your
    Stories highlight "Keepsakes." Low effort, high trust.
15. **Carousel of one full gallery wall from a real event** — mirrors the "Selected work"
    section of the site, works well as an evergreen profile-visit driver.
16. **Testimonial quote card** using an existing line (e.g. Clare & William) laid over a
    photo of their portrait. Static post + Story.

### Education

17. **Carousel: "Live painting vs. a photo booth vs. a caricature artist"** — a light,
    non-snarky comparison that positions watercolour portraiture as the premium option
    without naming competitors.
18. **Carousel: "What happens if my guest's portrait isn't finished on the night?"** —
    walks through the after-event studio finishing process. Answers the #1 objection quietly.
19. **Carousel: "How many people can actually get painted in 3 hours?"** — breaks down the
    ~8-pieces-per-hour math simply, ends on the package CTA.
20. **Carousel: "Cotton paper vs. regular paper"** — a close-up, tactile explainer on why
    archival cotton-rag paper matters for a keepsake that's meant to last decades.

### Offer

21. **"Now booking [Season/Year]"** static announcement — pairs with the design prompt
    in §5 below. Push to Stories with a countdown sticker for date scarcity.
22. **"From $850" package breakdown static/carousel** — plain-language version of the
    Packages section, one card per package tier.
23. **Add-on spotlight**: the wedding bouquet illustration ($500 A3) shown finished, since
    it's a distinctive add-on most competitors don't offer. Static post.
24. **Off-peak/quiet-month offer** (if applicable) to smooth booking seasonality — Stories
    only, kept low-key to avoid undercutting the no-hard-sell brand voice.

## 4. Caption and hashtag notes

- Keep captions in the site's voice: warm, plain, no exclamation marks, active voice,
  Australian spelling. Write like Chris talking, not like an agency.
- Every caption should end on either a soft CTA ("Enquiries open for 2027 dates — link in
  bio") or an emotional beat, never both stacked.
- Hashtag mix per post: 2–3 broad (`#weddingentertainment #liveweddingpainter
  #watercolourartist`), 2–3 local (`#melbourneweddings #sydneyweddings
  #melbourneweddingvendor`), 1–2 niche (`#liveweddingillustration #weddingkeepsake`).
  Skip generic spam tags (`#love #instagood`) — they don't move reach on Reels anymore.

## 5. Claude design prompts

screenshot at the right export size. Each one already encodes this project's design system —
Pastel Bloom palette, the no-grey-shadow rule, and the type stack (Darumadrop One for
display, Manrope for body, Mynerve for the handwritten accent) — so the output matches the
website without you having to redescribe the brand every time.

## 6. Which model to use

**For writing captions, hooks, and iterating on these design-prompt artifacts day to day:
use Claude Sonnet 5.** It's the right balance of quality and speed for this kind of
high-iteration creative work — generating a batch of caption variants, tweaking an HTML
carousel template, rewriting a hook five ways — and it already has this repo's design
system loaded via CLAUDE.md, so the output stays on-brand without extra prompting.

**For a small number of higher-stakes, final-polish pieces** — the pinned profile grid
layout, a flagship announcement graphic, or anything going into paid ads — step up to
**Claude Opus 4.8** for one careful pass. Its extra reasoning shows up as better composition
judgement and more consistent adherence to the anti-mud/shadow rules across a multi-slide
set, which matters more when the piece is permanent or paid than when it's one Reel in a
weekly cadence.

**For fast, disposable brainstorming** — a big batch of caption angles or hashtag sets to
skim and pick from — **Claude Haiku 4.5** is fast and cheap enough to run many variations
without waiting.

One honest limitation: Claude does not generate photorealistic images or video. The HTML
prompts above produce on-brand **graphic templates** (text layouts, quote cards, carousel
frames with placeholder photo slots) — you still drop in real event photography or portrait
scans yourself. For actual photo/video capture, keep using a phone or camera on the day;
Claude's job here is turning that raw footage and copy into a consistent, on-brand package
fast, not generating new imagery from scratch.
