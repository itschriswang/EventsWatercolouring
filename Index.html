I trained as an architect, then went back to painting people.

The approach That training never left. I build a portrait the way I once built a drawing, with composition first and restraint everywhere else. Then I let the water do the part I cannot control. The discipline is mine. The bloom is the paint's.

On the night Working live changes the room. Guests drift over to watch a face appear out of a wash, and by the last dance there is a painting carrying the feeling of the evening, made in front of the people who were in it.
