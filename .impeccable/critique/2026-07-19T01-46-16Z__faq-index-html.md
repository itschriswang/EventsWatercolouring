## Anti-patterns verdict

Not AI-slop. This is a committed, coherent brand system executed with restraint: opaque grained paper tiles floating over a single continuous watercolour wash (the wash reads only in the negative space between tiles), the orchid drop glyph as a consistent accent, warm plain-spoken Australian copy, and the three-font voice. The gradient-clipped wash on the eyebrow orchid and the Enquire CTAs is the deliberate brand identity, correctly not flagged. Motion is intentional and tiered (reduced-motion zeroing, pinch-zoom fallback), not uniform-reflex. The single real tell of machine output on this page is not styling but a contrast oversight (below), which is a fixable accessibility bug rather than generic-template slop.

## Cognitive load

2 of 8 fail. PASS: single focus (one clear job — pre-booking answers); chunking (each Q/A in its own tile); one-thing-at-a-time (all answers open, a deliberate and correct choice for FAQ); minimal choices; working memory (nothing to hold); progressive disclosure (intentionally none — appropriate). FAIL: grouping — 13 items in a single flat list with no categories (booking / on-the-day / logistics / materials would cluster naturally); visual hierarchy — the question heading (clamp max 1.15rem, normal weight, font-body) is only marginally larger than the answer and differentiated almost entirely by colour (ink vs ink-soft), so a 13-item list is slow to scan for the question you want.

## What's working

- The 'opaque paper cut-outs over one continuous wet wash' concept is genuinely original and well-executed: tiles are solid grained paper (bg-paper), so the bloom reads only in the gutters between them — a real watercolour metaphor, not a decorative gradient.
- Copy is excellent — plain, warm, specific, answers real pre-booking anxieties (retainer, postponement, travel radius, guests-per-piece maths) in the painter's own voice with no filler.
- Motion is properly tiered and intentional: staggered whileInView entrance capped at 5 items, zeroed under useReducedMotion, and an explicit pinch-zoom fallback (usePinchZoomed) so zoomed users aren't left with invisible mid-animation cards.
- Keeping the full Enquire reply card on the FAQ page (rather than bouncing the warmest possible reader back to the homepage) is a smart conversion decision, well-reasoned in the source comments.

## Priority issues

### [P1] FAQ answer body text fails WCAG AA contrast (4.0:1)
- **Why:** The answer paragraphs — which are the entire substance of the page — use text-ink-soft/85. On the opaque #F7F4EF paper card that computes to 4.0:1, below the 4.5:1 AA floor for normal text. Full ink-soft is already only 5.49:1, so the /85 dilution is what tips every answer on the page into failure. This is the muted-text-on-tinted-ground tell and it hits 100% of the primary content.
- **Fix:** Drop the /85 opacity so answers render at full ink-soft (#6B6065, 5.49:1). No palette change, no new colour — just remove the alpha. If a touch of visual softening vs the question is still wanted, get it from the question side (make the heading darker/heavier) rather than diluting the body below AA.
- **Command:** /impeccable colorize  (src/components/Faq.jsx:49)

### [P2] Weak question/answer typographic hierarchy across a 13-item list
- **Why:** The question h3 is font-body, normal weight, clamp(0.95rem–1.15rem) — barely larger than the answer and separated almost only by colour. In a flat 13-item list this makes scanning for a specific question slow; the eye can't lock onto question lines quickly.
- **Fix:** Give the question heading font-semibold (Manrope 600) and nudge its floor up (e.g. clamp(1.05rem,1.4vw,1.25rem)). This is on-brand — no font or colour change, just weight/scale — and turns the questions into clear scan anchors.
- **Command:** /impeccable typeset  (src/components/Faq.jsx:46)

### [P2] Centered card column clashes with the flush-left page composition
- **Why:** The list is max-w-3xl mx-auto (centered) while the hero header above and the Enquire section below are both left-aligned at px-[5vw]. On desktop this leaves a wide (~260px) empty band to the left of every card and makes the FAQ list read as a detached block rather than part of the page's left-anchored rhythm.
- **Fix:** Left-align the card column to the header's origin instead of centering it: replace mx-auto with the same left inset the hero/Enquire use (or wrap the whole hero+list in one shared max-width container). The wash still reads in the right-hand negative space, so the 'cut-outs over wash' effect is preserved while the composition regains a single left axis.
- **Command:** /impeccable layout  (src/components/Faq.jsx:26)

### [P3] Thirteen identical orchid glyphs and no grouping
- **Why:** Every card carries the same drop glyph, so the icon column adds visual repetition without wayfinding value, and 13 ungrouped questions is a lot to parse as one run.
- **Fix:** Either cluster the items into 3–4 labelled groups (Booking, On the day, The work, Logistics) with a small Mynerve sub-eyebrow per group, or drop the per-card glyph in favour of those group markers. Keeps the accent language but gives the list structure. Purely additive — no palette/shadow impact.
- **Command:** /impeccable layout  (src/components/Faq.jsx:39)

## Persona red flags

**Casey (mobile)**
- Answer text at ~4.0:1 is hardest to read exactly here — a narrow 390px column read one-handed, often outdoors in daylight.
- Questions are barely larger than answers, so scanning 13 items down a phone column is slow.
- The floating dock sits over the second card ('How far ahead should we book?') at the fold, partly occluding its answer while scrolling.

**Jordan (first-timer)**
- Low-contrast answers make the practical details (retainer %, travel radius, guest maths) a strain to read on first visit — the moment they most need clarity.
- The wide empty left gutter beside the centered cards can read as 'is content missing?' before the eye finds the list.

**Riley (stress-tester)**
- The longest answer ('How many guests can you paint?') is a dense ~90-word block with no internal breaks.
- A flat, uncategorised 13-item list with no in-page search or jump nav means locating one specific answer requires scrolling the entire list.

## Minor observations

- Hero intro paragraph correctly uses full text-ink-soft (5.49:1) — the contrast regression is isolated to the card answers' /85, confirming it's an oversight rather than a system choice.
- 'Send me a note' anchor is text-ink underlined — good affordance and contrast; keep it.
- No horizontal overflow at 390px; cards flex full-width and long answers wrap cleanly.
- Cards are non-interactive (all answers open), so there are no focus/keyboard affordance concerns in the list itself — appropriate.
- Generous top padding (clamp to 10rem) means the desktop fold shows only ~2 cards; acceptable editorial pacing but worth confirming it's intentional rather than lifting the list slightly.
