## Anti-patterns verdict

Not AI-slop. This reads as a genuinely authored site, not a template. The committed watercolour gradient-text ("painted", the Enquire CTAs) and the three-font system (Manrope / Mynerve / Darumadrop One) are protected brand identity and are executed with intent, not reflex. The tells that would betray a generator are absent: shadows are tinted per the Pastel Bloom rules (no grey), the palette holds its protected chartreuse voice, and the interactions are bespoke and expensive (coverflow lightbox, deckle-edge SVG paper, before/after wipe, the staged reply card with wax seal, the planner that carries chosen hours into the form). The one faintly generic reflex is that nearly every section enters on the same y:36 spring rise — but that sits inside a clearly authored motion system with distinctive per-section signatures (spine fill, word-by-word ink-dry quote, kit fan, seal press), so it does not read as slop.

## Cognitive load

0 of 8 hard fails — the page is well-managed, with two genuine near-misses. PASS: single focus (hero carries one CTA), chunking (folder sections, gallery split into two labelled 'rooms'), grouping, one-thing-at-a-time (3-step reply card), working memory (planner hours carry into the form), progressive disclosure (accordions, lightbox, stepped form). NEAR-MISS on visual hierarchy: a large family of same-weight mono uppercase eyebrows/labels compete for the same tier, and the smallest ones (0.54–0.55rem in the Mynerve handwritten face) strain the bottom of the scale. NEAR-MISS on minimal choices: the desktop Packages add-ons present all 8 options at once (the mobile accordion discloses them one at a time — the better pattern isn't carried to desktop).

## What's working

- Cohesive Pastel Bloom identity executed with unusual discipline: every shadow is a tinted burgundy/claret/deep-ink per the site rules (zero grey), the protected chartreuse accent survives, and deep anchors stay burgundy rather than drifting purple.
- Real narrative pacing — hero promise → the evening hour-by-hour (dusk folder) → the keepsake wall as proof → a couple's word right after the work is seen → the painter → decision → ask. The order does persuasive work, not just visual variety.
- Bespoke, genuinely tiered interactions: coverflow lightbox, drag/arrow before-after wipe, the reply card staged as three small answers, the planner that pushes chosen hours into the form. Each has a heavyFx/reduced-motion/lite fallback and thoughtful focus + a11y handling.
- Form UX is a standout: honeypot, real email validation with focus-to-field, aria-wired errors, date-min guard, and a graceful prefilled-mailto fallback when Formspree is unconfigured or the request fails.

## Priority issues

### [P2] Informational micro-copy dips below AA contrast on the bright ground
- **Why:** ink-soft is a comfortable 5.5:1 on paper, but the reduced-opacity variant used for real information fails: the hero reassurance note (text-ink-soft/85 at 0.6rem) computes to ~4:1, under the 4.5:1 needed for text that small. Reduced-opacity fades belong on decorative/aria-hidden marks, not on a line a buyer is meant to read. It is the classic 'muted text on tinted ground' legibility tell and it lands on a trust-building sentence.
- **Fix:** Render informational micro-copy at full ink-soft (drop the /85) so it holds 5.5:1, and reserve opacity fades for aria-hidden decoration only. While there, lift the smallest 0.54–0.55rem mono labels toward ~0.62rem so the Mynerve face stays legible. No palette change needed — ink-soft already passes at full strength.
- **Command:** /impeccable colorize  (src/components/Hero.jsx:196)

### [P2] Form placeholders carrying useful examples are near-invisible
- **Why:** Input placeholders use text-ink-soft/60 (~2.3:1), but several carry genuinely useful guidance — 'e.g. Melbourne' for venue, the phone format 'e.g. 0400 000 000', and the message prompt. At that contrast a first-timer on a phone in daylight won't read the format hint, which is exactly when they need it.
- **Fix:** Raise placeholder contrast to at least ink-soft/80, or promote the load-bearing examples (phone format, venue city) into a small helper line under the label at full ink-soft so the guidance survives regardless of placeholder styling.
- **Command:** /impeccable colorize  (src/components/EnquireForm.jsx:510)

### [P3] Package chips auto-advance with no confirmation or announcement
- **Why:** Tapping a package on step 1 immediately advances to step 2. A mis-tap is only undoable via Back, and because the sheet swap isn't announced, keyboard/screen-reader users can lose their place. It trades a small efficiency win for a control/orientation cost on the very first interaction of the form.
- **Fix:** Keep the auto-advance but wrap the stepped sheet in an aria-live=polite region that announces 'Step 2 of 3 — when and where', and set aria-current on the selected chip so the choice is confirmed before the jump. No visual change required.
- **Command:** /impeccable harden  (src/components/EnquireForm.jsx:406)

### [P3] The hero emphasis word 'painted' is the faintest element in the most important line
- **Why:** The pastel gradient on 'painted' is committed identity and must stay a gradient — but as rendered it is the lightest thing on a bright, actively-blooming ground, so the single word the whole headline hinges on reads faintest. The h1 className sets [text-shadow:none] across the element, which also removes the dark tinted drop the code comments describe as the thing the pastel letters are meant to 'lift off'.
- **Fix:** Preserve the gradient; restore the lift. Apply the approved dark tinted drop (e.g. a burgundy rgba(126,40,72,·) text-shadow) to the emphasis characters only rather than blanket-killing text-shadow on the whole h1, so the pastels separate from the ground. Optionally nudge the mid-arc stops a touch deeper within the palette (still on-arc) for weight. Verify the drop is present on both mobile and desktop line sets.
- **Command:** /impeccable polish  (src/components/Hero.jsx:177)

## Persona red flags

**Jordan (first-timer)**
- Above the fold there is no price or 'what's included' anchor — the cost model ('From $1000', then 8 add-ons all 'priced on enquiry') only surfaces far down the very long page, so a first-timer can't gauge fit early.
- The headline's load-bearing word 'painted' is the faintest element on screen, softening the one line meant to explain the offer.
- Desktop Packages fires all 8 add-ons at once with no disclosure, which reads as more to evaluate than a newcomer needs.

**Casey (mobile, 390px)**
- Faint guidance hits hardest on a phone: the ink-soft/60 placeholders ('e.g. Melbourne', phone format) and the 0.6rem/0.85-opacity hero note are the least legible exactly where daylight and small type compound.
- 0.54rem mono captions in the handwritten Mynerve face ('No. 001 · Cotton paper', planner footnotes) sit at the bottom edge of readability on a small screen.
- The before/after wipe depends on discovering a drag gesture; it's cued by a 'Drag to reveal' pill but that pill is itself tiny low-contrast mono.

**Riley (stress-tester)**
- Rapidly tapping package chips fires goto() on every tap and slides sheets with no debounce or confirm — easy to overshoot past the step you wanted.
- Pressing Continue on the when/where step with everything blank proceeds silently (intended optionality, but nothing signals it was allowed vs skipped).
- Placeholder examples are so faint that a tester will read them as empty fields and question whether formatting is validated (it isn't, beyond email).

## Minor observations

- Section-entrance motion is uniform: almost every block enters on the same y:36 SPRING rise. The distinctive per-section interactions carry the character, but the first reveal of each section could vary its direction/scale to feel less reflexive.
- alt text in content.js:112 describes a studio study as 'warm terracotta and ochre' — harmless as a description of an actual painting, but it's the one place the forbidden 'terracotta' word appears in author-facing copy; consider 'warm rust and ochre' to stay clear of the palette rule's language.
- Desktop hero leaves a large empty lower-right quadrant between the CTA block and the 'Scroll' cue — reads as intentional editorial air, but the balance tips slightly top-heavy.
- The mobile add-ons pattern (name visible, tap to disclose price/detail) is genuinely better than the desktop 8-up grid; porting that progressive disclosure to desktop would cut simultaneous choices.
- Lightbox images are loading=lazy with no low-res placeholder, so opening a piece on a slow connection can show a momentary blank card inside the coverflow.
