How it works

Two ways in. Both unhurried.

Live on the day

I come to your wedding and paint as it happens.

  1. 1We say helloYou tell me about the day, the venue, and the people who matter. A deposit holds your date.
  2. 2I paint in the roomI arrive early, set up quietly, and paint your guests through the celebration while they watch.
  3. 3Everyone takes one homeGuests leave with their own little painting. Your couple portrait is painted on the night and left with you.

A studio commission

From your photographs, before or after the wedding.

  1. 1Send me your photosA few well-lit, high-resolution images that look like you. I will help you choose the one to paint from.
  2. 2I paint in the studioI work from your images on cotton-rag paper, building the portrait by hand over the weeks that follow.

Questions

The practical bits.

I am taking bookings up to about two months out for now, so reach out with your date and I will let you know what I have open. Earlier is always welcome, as I only take a limited number of weddings.

Both. The base package has me at your wedding, painting live through the reception, and we build on it with add-ons to suit your day. If you would rather a studio commission from your own photographs, before or after the day, I am glad to do that too, priced on enquiry.

Three hours in the base package, and longer if you add hours, up to about five, usually through the reception with short breaks built in. I pace myself rather than paint flat out, so every piece stays as considered as the first.

Around 12 to 20 keepsake pieces depending on the package, covering roughly 24 to 40 guests when I group a few people together. I quote this as a chosen subset of the room, painted well, rather than a rushed attempt at everyone.

Professional watercolours on 300gsm A5 cotton-rag pads, Arches or similar, with lightfast pigments that hold their colour for decades when cared for. Everything is made to last.

Guest keepsakes are finished on the night and go home with your guests. Your couple portrait is painted on the night too and left with you as I finish. Studio commissions from photographs arrive within four weeks.

Travel is included for venues within about an hour of the Melbourne CBD by public transport. Anywhere further, including regional Victoria and destination weddings, I am glad to discuss, with travel quoted on enquiry. Just include your venue and I will let you know.

I do not frame pieces. Everything comes to you on beautiful cotton-rag paper, sleeved and ready for a framer you love. I am happy to suggest framers if that helps.

Yes. Every couple receives a high-resolution scan of their portrait to keep and reprint, for thank-you cards and the like. If you would like a file licensed specifically for stationery, just ask.

A 25% deposit holds your date, with the balance due four weeks before the wedding. I only confirm a booking once the deposit is received, so your date is yours from that point.

Weddings move, and I understand that. If you postpone, your deposit transfers to a new date subject to my availability. The deposit is non-refundable, as it reserves time I turn away other couples to hold. Full terms are set out in your booking agreement.

The clearer the photographs, the better the portrait. Send a few well-lit, high-resolution images that show your faces clearly, and let me know which one feels most like you. I will guide you from there.
